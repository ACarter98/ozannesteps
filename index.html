Walk the Path of History

A historic stone staircase leading from cliff top to sea, built in the 19th century and offering spectacular coastal views.

Guernsey Attraction

The Ozanne Steps represent a remarkable feat of 19th-century engineering, carved into the Guernsey cliff face to provide access to the sea below. Named after the Ozanne family who commissioned their construction, these steps have served fishermen, smugglers, and tourists for nearly 200 years.

Historical Significance

Built around 1820, the steps were originally constructed to allow fishermen to access the rich fishing grounds below the cliffs. Before their construction, reaching this part of the coast required dangerous descents over loose rocks. The steps also served a more illicit purpose - the secluded coves below were perfect for smuggling goods ashore away from customs officials in St Peter Port.

The Descent

Over 100 steps wind down the cliff face, offering increasingly spectacular views of the coastline as you descend. The steps are well-maintained but steep - not suitable for those with mobility issues or young children.

Modern Use

Today, the steps are popular with walkers exploring Guernsey's coastal path, anglers seeking secluded fishing spots, and photographers capturing the dramatic scenery. The effort of the descent is rewarded with one of the island's most peaceful and beautiful coves.

Visitor Tips

  • Not suitable for young children or mobility issues
  • Wear shoes with good grip - can be slippery
  • Bring everything you need - no facilities below
  • Allow 30 minutes for descent and ascent
  • Check tide times - cove cut off at high tide
